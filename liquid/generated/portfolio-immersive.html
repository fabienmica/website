From Virtual To Reality

To mark its 250th anniversary, the Royal Academy of Arts commissioned Jonathan Yeo to create Homage to Paolozzi, the first self-portrait sculpted in VR and cast in bronze. Google Arts & Culture produced a 360 ° film about the project. My business recreated Yeo ' s Chelsea studio in photorealistic 3D. The final video is available on the Google Arts & Culture YouTube channel.

Emilies (Les Temoins)

To launch season 2 of Les Témoins (Witnesses), France Télévisions created a virtual reality (VR) prequel titled Libérez Emilie. The experience, available as a VR escape room in Paris, required two players, one in VR and one in the physical room, to uncover the secret of Emilie, the killer's first victim.

My business developed the entire escape room experience, including the VR environment, interaction systems, and narrative logic:

  • We built a 1:1 virtual replica of the physical escape room, requiring precise spatial alignment between real-world objects and their digital counterparts
  • To enable seamless interaction without controllers, we developed MagnetAll, a custom calibration system that allowed the VR player to interact naturally with physical props and surfaces
  • We created Storymmersive, a dynamic storytelling engine that adapted the narrative in real time based on player decisions and timing, allowing for branching paths and last-minute script updates.
  • To visualize Emilie's presence, we engineered a custom particle system blended with motion capture data, delivering a haunting and emotionally resonant moment in the experience.

The result was a tightly integrated, cooperative VR experience that merged physical and virtual storytelling in a way that was both technically ambitious and narratively immersive.

Cinecittà World

My business recreated over 100 meters of street, including a dozen buildings, road, pavements, and signage, for Cinecitram, a ride at Cinecittà World theme park. Due to the immersive tunnel's high-resolution panoramic 3D projection, traditional filming wasn't feasible. Instead, the entire environment was captured and rebuilt in photorealistic 3D with detailed textures. Once completed, all assets were delivered for integration into the ride's animation system.

Research and Development

In the early days of my career, I worked in a start-up called Realviz and since acquired by Autodesk. It was a spin-off of INRIA the French National Instute in Computer Science and Applied Mathematics. I was then working in the nascent field of Computer Vision with the researchers who actually invented it.

Then I moved to Buf Compagnie where I oversaw the mathematical libraries of in-house software, and I implemented a complete C++ 3D reconstruction engine capable of photographs calibration (wide baseline), camera and 3D object tracking, match moving, optical motion capture. The engine is integrated in the studio's pipeline and was used for movie set reconstruction in The Matrix Reloaded and The Matrix Revolutions.
